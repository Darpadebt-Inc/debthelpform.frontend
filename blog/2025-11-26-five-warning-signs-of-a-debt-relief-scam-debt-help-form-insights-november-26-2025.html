Steps to take right now

  1. List every unsecured balance with interest rate, servicer, and days past due.
  2. Contact creditors to request hardship or forbearance options—log every call and keep copies of correspondence.
  3. Compare consolidation, settlement, and validation paths using certified counselors before you commit.
  4. Check your credit reports at annualcreditreport.com to confirm accuracy before negotiations start.
  5. Document any harassment for the CFPB or your state regulator so you can escalate quickly.

Signals that it's time to act

Quiet news doesn't mean creditors pause collections. Watch for these triggers:

  • Accounts charged off or sold to collections in the past 30 days.
  • New lawsuits, wage garnishment notices, or frozen bank accounts.
  • Stacked fees or interest rate hikes that make minimum payments unworkable.
  • Medical or tax debt layered on top of credit cards, personal loans, or payday balances.
